# Impeccable Design Context

> Generated from foundation doc interviews (2026-04-11) and design system bootstrap session (2026-04-12). This is the single design context file for Impeccable skills. All design work on this project should reference this before starting.

## Users

**Two personas, symmetric roles:**

- **The Organizer** — the partner who holds the mental load. Sought this app out. Motivated, organized, wants to dump what's in their head into a shared space so the invisible labor becomes visible. Opens the app 10+ times a day. Wants speed: type, enter, done.
- **The Willing Partner** — didn't seek this out. Got a link from someone they love. Not lazy, just didn't see the list. Needs a warm, zero-pressure entry. The "oh" moment — seeing what their partner has been carrying — is the emotional hook.

**Context of use:** At home, on the couch, in the kitchen, in bed, on the go. Domestic, daily, habitual. A couple running a life together. The app is opened in short bursts throughout the day — quick scans, quick actions, quick dopamine from checking things off. Morning triage, evening wind-down, Sunday planning.

**The job to be done:** Make invisible labor visible — gently, playfully, without blame. Coordination is table stakes. Recognition is the differentiator.

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** Crafted, knowing, warm.

- **Crafted** — premium without being corporate. Every detail is considered. The app feels like it was made by someone who cares, not generated by a machine. "Would I show this to a paying customer as an example of my work" is the quality bar.
- **Knowing** — the app gets you. It winks. It's playful the way a good friend is playful — dry, a little cheeky, occasionally a gentle tease. It doesn't explain itself too much because it trusts you to get it.
- **Warm** — on your side and on your partner's side simultaneously. A house you want to come home to. Never cold, never clinical, never scolding.

**Voice (theme-agnostic):** Warm, playful, cheeky, competent, low-ego. The cheeky accomplice helping two people run a life together. See `docs/voice-and-tone.md` for the full character description, do/don't examples, and the Cyberpunk test.

**The Cyberpunk test:** Every piece of copy must work identically in the warm Cozy theme AND in a hypothetical neon-magenta Cyberpunk theme. If the copy only works in one visual context, it's leaking theme into voice.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Default theme: "Cozy"**
Warm, playful, cheeky-cozy. The one Dave and Krista would actually live in daily. Not a showroom — a house you want to come home to.

**Visual tone:**
- Soft warm neutrals as the base. Nothing sterile, nothing clinical.
- A warm accent color — leaning into the peach/coral/terracotta/salmon family. Think the warmth of the Claude Cowork palette. Not neon, not pastel-to-the-point-of-invisible. Present but not screaming.
- Success in sage green. Warning in honey. Destructive in brick. Everything muted; nothing saturated to 100%.
- A very subtle paper/canvas texture on large surfaces. Nothing loud.
- Line icons with generous stroke weight and fully rounded corners. Never harsh, never thin.

**Typography direction:**
- A friendly, slightly rounded geometric or humanist sans for UI.
- A second typeface with personality for display moments — empty states, celebrations, onboarding headlines.
- NOT the reflex fonts (Inter, Plus Jakarta Sans, Fraunces, DM Sans, etc.). Follow the Impeccable font selection procedure to find something distinctive.

**Motion direction:**
- Smooth exponential deceleration — NOT bounce or elastic (per Impeccable guidelines).
- Task completion is celebrated with a brief, restrained flourish.
- Everything respects `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Optimistic UI everywhere — interactions feel instant.

**Iconography:** Line icons, generous stroke weight, fully rounded corners.

**Illustration:** Hand-drawn feel for empty states. Pastel fills. Gentle warm presence — not mascots, just warmth. Think: a good children's book illustration style, but not for children.

**Sound (post-v1, but token-ready):** Soft, warm, no harsh frequencies. Off by default.

**Theme:** Light. Couples using the app at home — kitchen counter, couch, bed. Light mode is the natural choice.

## Design Principles

1. **Themed from day one.** Every component uses theme tokens. No hard-coded values. The Cozy theme is the first answer to a token contract that future themes (Cyberpunk, Minimal, Bauhaus) will also answer.
2. **Today is the hero view.** Most users open the app to answer "what do I need to do right now?" Today gets 70% of the design energy. Week and Month are planning surfaces.
3. **Recognition over coordination.** When there's a design trade-off, pick the path that makes the other partner feel seen.
4. **Narrow surface, deep craft.** Fewer features, higher quality. Three things that feel like Linear beat ten that feel like a hackathon project.
5. **Accessibility is not optional.** WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard navigable. Screen reader labeled. Touch targets ≥ 44px. Every component, every time.
6. **Motion has meaning.** Animation reinforces cause and effect. Durations and easing are tuned, not framework defaults.
7. **Real states, not TODOs.** Every screen has real empty, loading, error states before a feature is considered done.

## Reference Apps

- **Things 3** — premium task management that doesn't feel like enterprise software. Beautiful animations, warm without being cutesy. The interaction quality bar.
- **Linear** — the craft and snappiness bar. Not the visual tone (too cold/corporate for a couples app).
- **Headspace** — warm, approachable, premium. Illustration and copy working together to create warmth without childishness.
- **Airbnb** — generous spacing, thoughtful typography, a feeling of care without fussiness.

## Anti-References

- **Todoist** — boring, sterile, dull. Functional but soulless. The app should never feel this plain.
- **Google Tasks** — too thin, too generic, disposable feeling.
- **Any Kanban board** — enterprise mental model, not domestic.
- **SaaS dashboards** — cards, metrics, blue accent. The opposite of warm and personal.
- Even with the most minimal/default theme, the app should never accept "plain" or "vanilla."

## Technical Constraints

- **Framework:** Next.js (App Router), React 19, TypeScript
- **Styling:** Tailwind CSS v4 (CSS-variable-first architecture, `@theme inline`)
- **Animation:** Framer Motion
- **Color space:** OKLCH for perceptual uniformity (per Impeccable guidelines)
- **Type scale:** Fixed `rem` scale for app UI (not fluid `clamp()`)
- **Spacing:** 4pt base grid (4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96)
- **Mobile-first**, responsive, PWA-quality on mobile
- **Desktop:** Left sidebar nav. **Mobile:** Bottom tab bar.

## Accessibility Requirements

- WCAG 2.1 AA contrast on all text and UI components
- Keyboard navigable: tab order, focus visible, roving tabindex where needed
- Screen reader labels on all interactive elements
- Touch targets ≥ 44px
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected in all animation
- No `user-scalable=no`
- Color never the sole indicator of state
